FAQs

  • What can I use this for?

    Tons of cool stuff. A blog, just some images that you like, maybe a personal website. It's flexible. You add content, we'll display it.

  • How do I use my own domain?

    You'll need to create a CNAME record pointing at smallvictori.es from your domain. There's a good guide written by Google on how to create a CNAME record. Bonus – this will also teach you a bit about how the internet works.

  • What this "markdown" thing?

    We're glad you asked! Markdown makes Small Victories much better by giving you an easy way to format your site. It's like HTML – a kind of "markup" language.
    Files that end in .md or .markdown will automatically be rendered into HTML and injected into your page. It's the same syntax Tumblr uses! See the original guide for more.

  • Can I use a root domain like example.com?

    Unfortunately, no. This is a limitation of DNS. We require you to make a CNAME record pointing at smallvictori.es, something you can't create on a root (apex) domain. However – you could just create it for www.example.com. That will work!

  • My site isn't updating!

    Uh-oh, that shouldn't be happening! First of all, make sure you have Dropbox turned on your computer and that the little green checkmark is next to the files in your smallvictori.es folder. All good? Then you'll want to wait a minute or two.
    If you still aren't seeing it update, feel free to email us and we will take a look.

Tips

  • Upload photos from your phone

    Remember, you can use any Dropbox app to interact with your folder. That means you could use the Dropbox iOS application to copy images from your phone straight to your website. Pretty neat, right?

    You could also do this with any text editing app that works with Dropbox, like iA Writer

  • Make it secret

    Don't want people visiting your page who don't know you?
    There's something programmers call "security through obscurity". It basically means that the less-guessable your domain is, the less likely someone will find it.
    So, make it crazy! And then, make sure you don't post it anywhere public on the internet – otherwise search engines (like Google) will know where it is. Unlike your Grandma, they don't forget.

  • Reverse the order of posts

    This is useful if you want to sort items in reverse chronology, like you would on a blog
    $(document).ready(function() { $(".span12").append($(".project") .get().reverse()); });

  • How do I make my site responsive?

    You can just insert the proper <meta> in the <head> by copying and pasting this script into your theme.js file:
    $(document).ready(function() { $('head').append("<meta name='viewport' content='width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0'>"); });

  • Use lazy loading if you have lots of images

    Grab a lazy-load plugin and put the .js file in your Small Victories folder. Then, throw $("img").lazyload(); into your theme.js file.
